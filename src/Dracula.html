Did You Know?

Dracula's name comes from the Romanian word "Dracul," meaning "dragon" or "devil." It was derived from his father, Vlad Dracul.

Vlad the Impaler, the historical inspiration for Dracula, was notorious for impaling his enemies and using fear as a weapon during battles.

Bram Stoker never visited Romania, yet he depicted Transylvania vividly in his 1897 novel, creating a lasting association with vampires.

Dracula's Castle, often identified as Bran Castle, was never actually home to Vlad the Impaler, though it remains a popular tourist destination.

Garlic, mirrors, and holy symbols are common vampire repellents popularized by Stoker's book and folklore traditions.
