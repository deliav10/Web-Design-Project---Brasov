Brașov is known for its medieval charm and stunning mountain views.

Welcome to Brașov

Brașov’s unique air is given by the numerous buildings built in the Renaissance, Baroque and Gothic style. This city in Transylvania has a well-preserved historic city centre, where you can revel in the view while enjoying the traditional food served at the many restaurants found on the city’s cobbled streets.

During the summer season there are many cultural and leisure events to be enjoyed. Just 30 km away from Brașov you can find Bran Castle, the historic building which inspired Bram Stoker when he wrote his novel Dracula.

Geography of Brașov

Brașov lies in the heart of Romania, positioned within the Brașov and Făgăraș depressions. The region's varied landscape ranges in elevation from 400 meters to 2,544 meters, with the city nestled at the base of the Piatra Mare and Postăvaru massifs.

Encircled by three hills—Tâmpa, Straja, and Dealul Cetății—Brașov enjoys a unique natural setting. Its neighboring regions include Harghita to the north, Covasna to the east, Prahova to the south, and Sibiu to the west.

Did You Know? Facts About Brașov

  • Between 1950-1960 Brașov was called the city of Stalin
  • The city was founded by Teutonic Knights in 1211 and first mentioned in documents as Brașov in 1251
  • The Black Church is the largest Gothic-style church in Transylvania.
  • The Council Square was once home to the Pillar of Infamy, where witches and those accused of wrongdoing were put on trial.
  • Poiana Brașov, located nearby, is one of the most popular ski resorts in Romania.
